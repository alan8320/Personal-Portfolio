Publications

Published a paper on "Manipulation of SIXAXIS Sensors for Interacting with Co-Existing Physical and Digital Entities in Mixed Reality" Read the paper in the International Journal of Applied Engineering Research

Click each title to view description
Problem Addressed
  • High Cost: MR systems typically require advanced sensors like environment understanding cameras, depth cameras, gaze tracking sensors, ultrasound, MEMS, IR enabled cameras, etc., which are expensive.
  • Complexity: The manipulation of data from these sensors necessitates complex algorithms, computer vision, and artificial intelligence, demanding high-end processing power.
  • Limited Accessibility: Due to the high costs and complexity, MR technology is not widely accessible to the masses.
Proposed Solution
  • Cost-Effective Sensors: The paper proposes using only SIXAXIS microelectromechanical sensors (accelerometer, gyroscope, and compass) to achieve similar functionality. These sensors are less expensive and already present in devices like gaming controllers.
  • Simpler Algorithms: It introduces a simpler algorithm for positioning and interacting with digital objects. This approach reduces the need for expensive and complex hardware.
  • Rectangular Mesh Model: Instead of using polygonal mesh models generated by depth sensing cameras, a rectangular mesh model is used for spatial mapping, which simplifies the processing and reduces costs.
  • Relative Positioning Algorithm: This algorithm allows for the placement and tracking of digital objects relative to a calibrated origin without needing an absolute physical reference, making it versatile for various environments.
